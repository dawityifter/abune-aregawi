# Debre Tsehay Abune Aregawi Church Platform — Demo Script (English)
### Video Recording Script · ~12 Minutes

---

## SECTION 1 — The Home Page
**Screen: Home Page (not logged in)**

---

Welcome to Debre Tsehay Abune Aregawi Tigray Orthodox Tewahdo Church.

This is our digital home. Everything a visitor or member needs is right here on this page.

At the top, you'll see three quick-action buttons. **Give Online** — for online donations. **View Our YouTube Channel** — for live and recorded services. And **Join us on WhatsApp** — to connect directly with our community group.

Just below the hero, if a service is livestreaming right now, a live banner will automatically appear here. No one has to manually post anything — it's always live.

Scroll down and you'll find our **Interactive Calendar**. It displays all seven major fasts and nine major feasts of the Tigray Orthodox calendar — sourced directly from the authoritative **ቦክረ ሊቃዉንት መምህር አፈወርቅ** church calendar. Members always know what's coming up in the church year.

Further down is our **Announcements** section — rotating messages from church leadership with the latest news and events.

At the bottom, our footer has links to **Facebook**, **Instagram**, **YouTube**, and the **WhatsApp group** — all in one place.

---

Now — one of our most important features. Watch the top corner.

**[ Toggle language to Tigrigna ]**

The entire page — every word, every button, every label — switches to Tigrigna. This is not an automatic translation. Every phrase was written by hand, in our language, by our community. Our elders and members who are not comfortable in English can use this platform fully.

**[ Toggle back to English ]**

## SECTION 2 — Member Registration
**Screen: Sign In Page → Registration Flow**

---

To access the platform, we click the **Sign In** link. This takes us to the login page, where we enter our phone number.

If the phone number is already in our system, the member is logged in directly. But if it's a new number — the system recognizes this is a new person and automatically redirects them into the registration flow.

The registration process has seven steps. We ask for your **full name**, **phone number**, **address**, **family members**, **spiritual information** like your baptism name and fasting preferences, and finally your **annual pledge**.

We use your **phone number** as your identity — not an email — because a phone number is tied to a real person, and it's what we use for all our SMS communications.

Once you finish, your account is created and you're taken to your personal dashboard.

---

## SECTION 3 — The Member Dashboard
**Screen: Member Dashboard**

---

This is the **Member Dashboard**. Think of it as your personal command center.

You'll notice the dashboard is smart — it only shows you the tools relevant to your role. A regular member sees different cards than a treasurer or administrator.

Let's look at what every member has:

**My Profile** — View and edit your personal information at any time.

**My Dues** — See exactly where you stand with your monthly membership dues. How much you've paid, how much is outstanding, month by month. No need to call anyone.

**My Family** — As a head of household, you can add your spouse and children here. The church keeps accurate family records, and your dependents can eventually create their own member accounts when they're ready.

**Make a Donation** — A member can give a one-time or recurring donation directly from here, using a credit card or bank transfer.

**Departments** — Browse all church departments, see which ones you belong to, and access department news and meetings.

**Church Bylaws** — Our full bylaws document, available in both English and Tigrigna, always accessible from your dashboard.

**Photo Gallery** — Church event photos, organized by album.

---

## SECTION 4 — Announcements & Church TV
**Screen: Outreach Dashboard → Announcements**

---

Our **Announcements** feature is managed by the Relationship team or administrators.

Creating an announcement is simple. You write a title and body in English. And if you want — you can also write the Tigrigna translation right here, side by side. The system stores both.

**[ Show the TV View button ]**

Now here's something unique — the **Church TV View**. This is a full-screen, rotating display designed to be shown on a projector or TV screen inside the church before and after services.

Each announcement slides in automatically, pauses, and transitions to the next. If an announcement has a Tigrigna version, it shows both — one after the other. Members can read the news in their own language as they gather.

---

## SECTION 5 — The Treasurer Dashboard: Overview
**Screen: Treasurer Dashboard → Overview tab**

---

The Treasurer has a dedicated, secure dashboard.

The first thing you see is the **Overview**. At a glance: the current bank balance, total income this year, total expenses this year, and our net position.

Below that is the **Annual Dues Progress** — showing how many members are up to date with their dues, how many are behind, and the total outstanding balance.

**[ Use the year selector ]**

And this year selector at the top lets you look at any past year. If leadership wants to review 2024 or 2025, they simply select it here and all numbers update immediately.

---

## SECTION 6 — Payments & Expenses
**Screen: Treasurer Dashboard → Payments tab**

---

The **Payments** tab shows every income transaction recorded in the system — cash, check, Zelle, card, and ACH.

To record a new payment, we click **Add Payment**. We select the member, the payment type — membership due, tithe, donation, offering, or other — the amount, the date, and the receipt number.

For membership dues, notice there is a **year selector**. If someone is paying their 2025 dues today in 2026, we select 2025 here. The payment is applied to the correct year.

The **receipt number** is required for cash and check payments. And the system enforces that every receipt number is unique — no accidental duplicates. If no receipt was given in the moment, the cashier can enter **000** as a placeholder, and the system will show a warning reminding them to issue the receipt as soon as possible.

---

Now the **Expenses** tab.

This is where the treasurer records every church expense — building costs, supplies, visiting priests, and more.

When adding an expense, we choose from our expense categories. For categories like **Visiting Priest Allowances and Travel**, the system asks for a **payee name** — a free text field — because visiting priests are not in our employee list.

Check numbers for expenses are also unique — the system prevents accidentally recording the same check twice.

---

## SECTION 7 — Zelle Review
**Screen: Treasurer Dashboard → Zelle Review tab**

---

Many of our members send their dues and offerings through Zelle. The **Zelle Review** tab makes reconciling those payments straightforward.

The system reads incoming Zelle emails directly from Gmail. Every unprocessed Zelle payment appears here — the sender, the amount, the date, and a suggested member match based on the memo or phone number.

The treasurer reviews each one. If the match is correct, they confirm it. If not, they can search for the right member by name.

For membership dues, a **year dropdown** appears right here. If the payment is for 2025 dues, the treasurer selects 2025 — and the transaction is recorded against that year, not the current one.

Multiple payments can be selected and processed in one batch click. Once processed, they disappear from the queue.

---

## SECTION 8 — Member Dues Lookup
**Screen: Treasurer Dashboard → Member Dues tab**

---

The treasurer can look up any individual member's full payment history in seconds.

Search by name, select the member, and see every payment — the date, method, amount, receipt number, and which year it was for. If a member calls and says "Did my check clear?" — the answer is right here.

---

## SECTION 9 — Bank Reconciliation
**Screen: Treasurer Dashboard → Bank Reconciliation tab**

---

For formal bank reconciliation, the treasurer uploads a CSV export from the bank.

The system reads each line — the amount, date, and description — and tries to match it to a transaction already in the system. Matched items are marked. Unmatched ones are flagged for review.

This keeps our books clean and gives us a full audit trail linking every bank transaction to a recorded entry.

---

## SECTION 10 — Admin: Members & Communications
**Screen: Admin Dashboard**

---

The **Admin Dashboard** gives church administrators full control.

The **Members** tab shows the complete member list. You can search, filter, and click any member to edit their profile — name, contact, address, role, or spiritual information. New members can be added manually here as well.

The **Role Management** tab controls who has access to what. Assigning someone as a treasurer gives them the full treasurer dashboard. Assigning them as secretary gives them meeting management. Each role sees exactly what they need — nothing more.

---

**Screen: SMS Broadcast**

---

The **SMS Broadcast** tool sends text messages directly to members' phones.

You can send to a single member, an entire department, all active members, or members who have not yet fulfilled their annual pledge. Type your message, preview the recipient list, and send.

Members receive it on their personal phone — no app required on their end.

---

**Screen: Voicemail Inbox**

---

Our church has a dedicated phone number. When a member calls and leaves a message, it appears here in the **Voicemail Inbox**.

Leadership can play the recording, read the automated transcription, and archive it once it's been handled. Every voice message from our community is heard and recorded.

---

## SECTION 11 — Secretary: Meetings & Tasks
**Screen: Department Dashboard → Meetings**

---

For our secretaries and department leaders, the platform includes a full **Meeting Management** system.

When creating a meeting, the agenda and minutes can be written in both English and Tigrigna — side by side. When minutes are shared with the congregation, everyone reads them in their preferred language.

During the meeting, decisions become **Tasks**. We assign a task to a specific person, set a deadline, and mark its priority. At the next meeting, the system shows which tasks from last time are still open, in progress, or completed.

This creates a permanent record. Years from now, church leadership can look back and see exactly what was decided, who was responsible, and when it was done.
